THE PROBLEM

Labor shortages and high turnover

High demand for workers, but a continually depleting supply of them.

Employees stretched too thin

Countless firsthand accounts of nurses and hospital staff across the world being overworked during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Physically demanding and dangerous tasks

There are over 10 million unsafe or undesirable jobs in the US alone, with many involving moving heavy items or handling dangerous inventory.

Massive gaps in the labor market

There are upwards of 8.8 million unfilled jobs in the United States, with more than 4 million Americans quitting their job each month.

Existing solutions miss the mark

Either too ambitious or too formulaic.

Academia isn't focused on practicality

Researchers are more interested in exploring end-to-end learning and autonomy. Most lack the data necessary to implement a real-world solution.

Moonshots are trying to achieve the impossible

Industrial robots are overly formulaic

Robots in industry need low variability and high repeatability. They require human operators and are limited to very specific contexts (e.g. pick-and-place).

OUR APPROACH

The middle ground

A more reusable and adaptable robotics system, focused on simple and reliable tasks.

Easy to use

Train by feeding in simple + informative 1st-person videos of tasks.

Human-first design

Allows for corrective human input: tagging important objects, further guiding with ChatGPT-style prompts.

Reliable and reusable

Focused on skills that are transferrable across industries and easily taught through video.

Deploy a trained robot instantly by feeding a single video.

Step 1: Select a demo video

Choose the reference video you want to mimic. Our preprocessing engine extracts all the important objects and relevant actions.

Step 2: Customize what the robot learns

Use our intuitive UI to select the most important interaction points. Add optional GPT-style guiding prompts to further fine-tune robot action.

Step 3: Deploy anywhere

Reproduce the same task in any environment using our modular parts and adaptive learning models.
